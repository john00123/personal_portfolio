Overview

In 2016 I had the opportunity to explore design solutions outside of our product portfolio. Nutanix Smart Bezel was the result of that exercise. At its core the project created an abstraction that allowed communication between server infrastructure, our installation and management platforms through a combination of hardware and software.

The problem

Nutanix hardware installation process was fragmented. Datacenter admins needed to keep track on the inventory and the state of each piece of hardware through an excell sheet, retype network information manually to another platform and use this same information to register the product. An inefficient and costly process to company, partners and our customers that was prone to human error as it relies on administrators to keep track of all moving parts.


The challenge

Nutanix during that period provided only on-premises solutions. For the vast majority of our clients that meant that no communication outside the datacenter for security purposes.

To create a secure channel I needed a way to establish a local connection between the hardware and the system, for this reason using a companion app as the orchestration layer was a core part of this concept. It was portable, accesible and easy to develop a prototype for a POC.

After pitching the idea to my colleagues we decided to utilize a piece of hardware that traditionally gets removed from the chasis, the bezel of the node as the missing piece to link the hardware and our app.

Time to refresh

During the company's hackathon we had time to decipher the details of how to make this work. I posted at that moment our 3 designers team in the list of project participating. A couple of hours later we had 3 engineers interested in helping develop this concept further. We started by designing the newly conceptualized bezel. We expanded it's functionality to be able to display information back to the customer through a 255 color strip.


Our next goal was to create the app that would be used as a conduit between our hardware inventory, imaging and management platforms.
Our proof of concept consisted in registering a node to our platform, run an update action and having the node show an update status through its LED strip.

Opportunity

It was during the concept phase for the companion app in which I thought to leverage some of the smartphone functionality to enhance the concept even further by detecting the relative positioning of the phone during a registration event. This will allow the user to create a 3D space of the server room, information that can be use to make hardware troubleshooting more efficient. The techinical details of how we achieve this are listed in our patent application. It was this idea and the work of the team that led the project to win a nomination to the UX awards in Berlin during IFA event floor.
