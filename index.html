In 2015 I had the opportunity to explore design solutions outside of our product portfolio, Nutanix Smart Bezel was the result of that exercise. At its core this faceplate was designed to allow communication between the server infrastructure and Nutanix management platform (Prism).

The problem

The complete hardware installation process is fragmented across several interfaces today. Admins need to manually keep track on the inventory and the state of each piece of hardware through an excell sheet. Imaging happens through Nutanix Foundation platform and registration happens through Prism. If there are inconsistencies in the data, validation happens manually.

In cases of hardware performance issues, datacenter admins in some cases rely on second hand information in order to pin point a malfunctioning devices.

Both cases showcase a time consuming experience that naturally leads to human mistakes.

Why a faceplate

Nutanix was originally providing on premise solutions, that meant that no communication outside the datacenter for security reasons. Some companies use barcodes, but this solution requires dedicated hardware and systems. In order to be able to establish a channel we needed a device connected to the local network and some basic registration information from the hardware encoded in a nfs plate. Most phones are able to read data from NFR sensors so creating a companion app was part of the initial concept.



The solution

This could significantly reduce human error during the inventory and registration, reduce the time on task and create a communication channel between customer and platform that extended the possibilities of what could be achieved* at a relatively low cost.
